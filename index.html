About Me

Yasmeena Akhter is a Kashmiri-born researcher currently holding a Visiting Researcher position at MBZUAI. She works with Professor Marcos Matabuena in the Department of Epidemiology.

She completed her PhD in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur, Rajasthan, India. She worked with Professor Mayank Vatsa and Professor Richa Singh at the Image Analysis and Biometrics Lab, IIT Jodhpur.

Her research focuses on developing efficient deep learning frameworks through a multimodal approach, with emphasis on lightweight and interpretable model architectures for accurate automated diagnosis under computational and infrastructural constraints.

Research interests: pattern recognition, interpretable deep learning, healthcare AI, medical imaging, and trusted AI.

Current focus

Democratizing advanced diagnostic AI and bridging the gap between state-of-the-art AI research and underserved healthcare systems globally.
